# Executive Sales Strategy Recommendation

## 1. Strongest Growth Drivers
Based on a multiple regression analysis explaining 82% of sales variance (R-squared = 0.819), the strongest structural drivers of monthly sales are **Store Location Type** and **Inventory Availability**. Specifically, holding all operational metrics equal, Airport locations generate a massive $115,216 monthly premium over standard Residential stores, while High Street locations generate a $39,912 premium. 

## 2. Variables for Leadership Focus
* **Inventory Availability:** Every 1% drop in inventory availability costs the company $1,481 in monthly sales per store. Supply chain logistics must be prioritized to maintain 99%+ availability.
* **Footfall:** Footfall drives a steady $63.63 per visitor. Strategies to passively increase traffic (e.g., window displays, exterior merchandising) directly correlate to top-line revenue.

## 3. Variables to Re-evaluate (Warning)
* **Marketing Spend:** The model exposes a severe inefficiency in marketing. The coefficient is 0.29, meaning the company only receives $0.29 in sales for every $1.00 spent on marketing. **Do not scale the current marketing strategy.** It is actively generating a negative ROI and destroying capital. 

## 4. Recommended Business Actions
1. **Audit Marketing ROI:** Halt and audit all localized marketing spend immediately. Reallocate that budget into supply chain optimization to guarantee maximum inventory availability across the network.
2. **Real Estate Strategy:** Prioritize future real estate expansion in Airports and High Streets. Consider closing or relocating underperforming Residential locations, as they suffer a severe baseline mathematical disadvantage.
3. **Investigate Outliers:** Residual analysis identified specific stores outperforming their mathematical baseline by over $100,000 in a single month (e.g., Observation #888). Leadership should dispatch regional managers to these specific stores to document and scale their unmeasured best practices (e.g., exceptional store management or localized sales tactics).

## 5. Risks, Limitations & Causation Warning
* **Correlation vs. Causation:** Regression proves mathematical association, not direct causation. For example, Airport stores have high sales coefficients, but opening a store in an abandoned airport will not magically generate $115k. The coefficient captures the *typical* high-wealth, high-volume environment of an active transit hub.
* **Limitations:** The model does not account for qualitative data (e.g., customer service quality, local macroeconomic conditions, or competitor pricing). Leadership decisions should pair this statistical baseline with ground-level operational context.
